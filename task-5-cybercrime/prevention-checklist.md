# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

## Actionable Tips:

1. **Use Strong Passwords**: Create complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using common phrases or personal information.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication**: Opt for two-factor authentication on your accounts to add an extra layer of security beyond just a password.

3. **Beware of Phishing Scams**: Do not click on suspicious links or attachments in emails or messages. Verify the sender before sharing sensitive information.

4. **Use Secure Wi-Fi Connections**: Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions. Use a VPN if you must connect to a public network.

5. **Monitor Your Bank Statements**: Regularly check bank statements and UPI transaction histories for unauthorized transactions. Report any discrepancies immediately.

6. **Keep Software Updated**: Ensure that your computer and mobile devices have the latest security updates installed to protect against vulnerabilities.

7. **Educate Yourself on UPI Safety**: Only use UPI apps from trusted sources. Regularly review your transaction history and set transaction limits for added security.

8. **Know the Reporting Channels**: Familiarize yourself with reporting channels like the Cyber Crime Cell (https://cybercrime.gov.in) and your bank’s customer service for fraud alerts.

9. **Limit Personal Information**: Be cautious about the information you share on social media. Adjust privacy settings to safeguard your identity.

10. **Backup Important Data**: Regularly backup your important files to avoid loss due to malware or hacking attempts.

By following these simple steps, students can better protect themselves from online threats and ensure their safety in the digital world.
